**RE:** Cellphone Plan Cost Audit — Findings and Recommendations

---

## Background

A review of departmental cellphone plan assignments was conducted for 20 employees
across Finance, Engineering, Planning, Maintenance, Administration, IT, and Safety.
Current combined monthly expenditure totals **$1,252** ($15,024 annually), distributed
across three carrier plan tiers:

| Plan Tier | Monthly Rate | Data Allowance | Employees |
|-----------|-------------|----------------|-----------|
| Basic     | $40         | 2 GB           | 5         |
| Standard  | $60         | 5 GB           | 8         |
| Premium   | $80         | 15 GB          | 7         |

---

## Methodology

Usage records — including monthly data consumption (GB), voice minutes, and any
overage charges incurred — were obtained and reviewed for each assigned device.
Plan utilization was calculated as actual data usage relative to each plan's included
allowance. Employees utilizing less than **30% of their plan's included data**
were flagged as downgrade candidates. Employees generating overage charges were
flagged for upgrade to eliminate ongoing cost exposure.

---

## Finding 1 — Five Employees Are Significantly Overpaying for Unused Capacity

The following employees are assigned to plan tiers that far exceed their demonstrated
monthly usage:

| Employee          | Department     | Plan           | Data Used | Utilization | Monthly Savings |
|-------------------|----------------|----------------|-----------|-------------|-----------------|
| Sarah Mitchell    | Finance        | Premium ($80)  | 0.8 GB    | 5%          | $40             |
| Robert Landry     | Finance        | Premium ($80)  | 0.5 GB    | 3%          | $40             |
| David Leblanc     | Engineering    | Premium ($80)  | 0.7 GB    | 5%          | $40             |
| Mark Poulin       | Administration | Premium ($80)  | 0.6 GB    | 4%          | $40             |
| Mary Violette     | Administration | Standard ($60) | 1.0 GB    | 20%         | $20             |

Each of these employees consumes well within the 2 GB threshold of the Basic plan ($40/mo).
Downgrading them requires no change to their devices, numbers, or day-to-day use.

**Gross downgrade savings: $180/month — $2,160/year**

---

## Finding 2 — Two Employees Are Generating Recurring Overage Charges

Two employees are assigned to plans that do not cover their actual usage, resulting
in monthly overage billing:

| Employee           | Department  | Current Plan    | Data Used | Overage/Mo | Action            |
|--------------------|-------------|-----------------|-----------|------------|-------------------|
| Patricia Ouellete  | Maintenance | Standard ($60)  | 5.8 GB    | $8.00      | Upgrade → Premium |
| Thomas Michaud     | Maintenance | Basic ($40)     | 2.4 GB    | $4.00      | Upgrade → Standard|

Upgrading these employees to the next plan tier eliminates unpredictable overage
charges, simplifies invoice reconciliation, and right-sizes their plans to actual use.

**Net additional cost from upgrades: $28/month — $336/year**
(Premium flat rate of $80 replaces effective $68; Standard flat rate of $60 replaces effective $44)

---

## Recommendations

1. **Downgrade** Mitchell, Landry, Leblanc, Poulin, and Violette to the Basic plan,
   effective the next billing cycle. No device changes are required.

2. **Upgrade** Ouellete to the Premium tier and Michaud to the Standard tier to
   eliminate recurring overage billings and normalize their monthly invoices.

3. **Establish a semi-annual plan review** aligned with carrier billing cycles.
   As roles evolve, so does data usage — a scheduled review prevents plan drift
   from becoming a recurring budget issue.

---

## Financial Impact Summary

| Action                         | Monthly Change | Annual Change  |
|-------------------------------|---------------|---------------|
| 5 employee downgrades         | −$180         | −$2,160       |
| 2 employee upgrades           | +$28          | +$336         |
| **Net savings**               | **−$152**     | **−$1,824**   |

Implementing these recommendations reduces total annual cellphone expenditure
by approximately **12.1%** — from $15,024 to $13,200 — with no reduction in
service quality or employee capability.
